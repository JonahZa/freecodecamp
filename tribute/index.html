Linus Torvalds



Linus Torvalds is best known for initiating the development of the Linux kernel, a foundational component of the Linux operating system.

Born in Finland, Torvalds released the first version of the Linux kernel in 1991, which has since become a cornerstone of the open-source software movement.

His collaborative approach to development, fostering a global community of contributors, has led Linux to be adopted across a myriad of devices, from servers and desktops to embedded systems and supercomputers.

Beyond his contributions to the kernel, Torvalds' leadership has shaped the ethos of open-source development, emphasizing collaboration, transparency, and community-driven innovation.
